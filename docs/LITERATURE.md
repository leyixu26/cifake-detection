## 1. The foundational OOD problem (pre-CLIP)

These papers established that **spatial CNN detectors generalize poorly
across generators**. They are the baseline that CLIP-based methods improved
on, and they motivate the entire inductive-bias framing the team is using.

### Wang, Wang, Owens, Efros — CVPR 2020
*"CNN-generated images are surprisingly easy to spot... for now"*

- **Setup:** Trained a single ResNet-50 binary classifier on **ProGAN** images only, then evaluated on 10 unrelated generators (BigGAN, StyleGAN, CycleGAN, StarGAN, GauGAN, CRN, IMLE, SITD, SAN, deepfake portraits).
- **Headline finding:** With appropriate data augmentation (JPEG, blur, cropping), the ProGAN-trained detector generalizes to *most* other generators with >90% AP, despite never seeing them in training.
- **What it actually shows:** spatial features have *some* generalization, but only with aggressive train-time augmentation. Without augmentation, cross-generator performance drops to 50-60% AP on many generators.
- **Why it matters for us:** introduces the **ForenSynths benchmark** still widely used; establishes the cross-generator OOD evaluation protocol; demonstrates the augmentation crutch that frozen-CLIP approaches don't need.

### Frank et al. — ICML 2020
*"Leveraging Frequency Analysis for Deep Fake Image Recognition"*

- **Setup:** DCT-based features + simple classifier on GAN-generated images.
- **Headline finding:** GAN upsampling produces characteristic grid-like artifacts visible in the DCT spectrum; a classifier on DCT coefficients separates real/fake with >99% accuracy in-distribution.
- **Caveat (and why we found weak frequency results):** these results are at 128×128+ resolution where upsampling-grid peaks are well-resolved. At 32×32 (CIFAKE) the artifacts are aliased and the signal is much weaker.
- **Connects to:** the frequency probe in this project (Model 4) was motivated directly by this paper.

### Corvi, Cozzolino, Zingarini, Poggi, Nagano, Verdoliva — ICASSP 2023
*"On the detection of synthetic images generated by diffusion models"*

- **Setup:** Tested existing GAN-detection methods on diffusion-generated images (LDM, GLIDE, DALL-E 2).
- **Headline finding:** Diffusion models leave fingerprints similar to GANs but **softer and more sensitive to post-processing** (JPEG, blur). Existing GAN-detection methods transfer poorly to diffusion without retraining.
- **Why it matters for us:** motivates the robustness battery we ran (JPEG/blur destroy the frequency signal in our experiments); diffusion fingerprints exist but are more fragile than GAN fingerprints.

## 2. CLIP for fake image detection — the central thrust

The papers below establish the **frozen CLIP + linear/MLP probe** recipe as
the current state of the art for generator-agnostic AI image detection.
This is the literature foothold for Model 5 in our project.

### Ojha, Li, Lee — CVPR 2023 (the seminal paper for our approach)
*"Towards Universal Fake Image Detectors that Generalize Across Generative Models"*

- **Setup:** Pre-trained CLIP ViT-L/14 image encoder, **frozen**. Trained a linear logistic regression on top using fake images from **ProGAN only** as training data. Evaluated on 19 generators (mix of GANs and diffusion: ProGAN, CycleGAN, BigGAN, StyleGAN, GauGAN, StarGAN, Deepfake, SITD, SAN, CRN, IMLE, Guided, LDM-200, LDM-100, LDM-200_cfg, GLIDE-100-27, GLIDE-50-27, GLIDE-100-10, DALL-E).
- **Headline finding:** Frozen CLIP + linear probe **substantially outperforms** end-to-end fine-tuned ResNet-50, including Wang et al. 2020's augmentation-heavy detector. The numbers (averaged over the 19 test generators):
  - **Frozen CLIP + linear probe: ~95% mean AP** across generators
  - Wang et al. 2020 (ResNet-50 + heavy augmentation): ~78% mean AP
  - Other end-to-end methods: 60-80% range
- **Three additional findings worth citing:**
  1. **Nearest-neighbor (no training) on CLIP features works almost as well as linear probe.** Suggests the geometric structure of CLIP space, not the trained head, is doing most of the work.
  2. **Adding capacity to the probe (deeper MLPs) hurts cross-generator transfer** even when it helps in-distribution. The linear probe is preferred.
  3. **ImageNet-pretrained backbones (ResNet, ViT) underperform CLIP** when used the same way — the gain is specifically from CLIP's contrastive image-text pretraining objective, not just from "any pretrained encoder."
- **Limitations:** all experiments at ≥256×256 (vs our 32×32); only trained on ProGAN images (not diffusion); doesn't test the LAION-weights variant.
- **What we extended:** We tested at 32×32 (where the artifact is harder), trained on diffusion images, used LAION-2B weights (not OpenAI), and found that an MLP head with dropout helps rather than hurts our OOD performance — partially contradicting Ojha's finding (3), likely because we have more training data (90k vs their smaller set).

### Cozzolino, Poggi, Corvi, Nießner, Verdoliva — CVPRW 2024
*"Raising the Bar of AI-generated Image Detection with CLIP"*

- **Setup:** Direct successor to Ojha. Pre-trained CLIP encoder, linear or shallow MLP probe, trained on a mix of GAN + diffusion images.
- **Headline finding:** With careful training-set composition (multiple generators rather than ProGAN only) and minimal probe capacity, frozen-CLIP detectors achieve **>95% accuracy across 19 generators including the latest diffusion models (SD-XL, Midjourney v5)**.
- **Findings useful for our report:**
  - Confirms Ojha's frozen-CLIP recipe at modern (2023) generator scale
  - Demonstrates that the recipe **doesn't lose performance when extended to diffusion-only training data** — relevant since we trained on SD-1.4 only
  - Notes that **adding any amount of fine-tuning to the CLIP encoder generally hurts cross-generator transfer**, confirming the freeze-only choice
- **Numbers:** ~96% mAP across generators, vs ~80-85% for end-to-end methods.

### Sha, Li, Li, Zhang, Zhang — ACM CCS 2023
*"DE-FAKE: Detection and Attribution of Fake Images Generated by Text-to-Image Generation Models"*

- **Setup:** Used CLIP image and text encoders to both **detect** AI images and **attribute** them to the specific generator (Stable Diffusion vs Latent Diffusion vs GLIDE vs DALL-E).
- **Headline finding:** A simple classifier on CLIP features achieves >90% detection AUROC and >80% attribution accuracy across 4 modern text-to-image generators.
- **Why it matters for us:** demonstrates that CLIP features encode **generator-specific** information, not just "AI vs real" — supports the broader inductive-bias argument that pretraining diversity matters.
- **Bonus:** they release **DE-FAKE dataset** (large-scale text-to-image fakes paired with captions) — could be used as a future OOD probe.

## 3. Why CLIP works (and what about alternatives?)

### Radford et al. — ICML 2021 (the original CLIP paper)
*"Learning Transferable Visual Models From Natural Language Supervision"*

- **Setup:** Trained on 400M image-text pairs scraped from the web, using a contrastive objective: pull matching pairs together, push non-matching apart.
- **Headline finding for our purposes:** the learned visual features transfer to *30+ downstream tasks* without fine-tuning, often beating fully-supervised baselines.
- **Specifically relevant claim:** representations learned by image-text contrastive training are **more general** than representations learned by ImageNet classification, because the latter is constrained by the 1000-class label structure.

### Schuhmann et al. — NeurIPS 2022
*"LAION-5B: An open large-scale dataset for training next generation image-text models"*

- **Setup:** Released **LAION-5B**, a 5-billion image-text pair dataset; demonstrated that CLIP models trained on a 2B subset match or exceed OpenAI's original CLIP (trained on 400M proprietary pairs).
- **Why it matters for us:** **explains why LAION-2B weights beat OpenAI weights in our experiments** (+1.7 pp OOD). The LAION dataset is larger and more diverse than OpenAI's, and the resulting features are correspondingly more transferable.
- **Headline number:** on standard transfer benchmarks (ELEVATER, ImageNet linear probe), LAION-trained CLIPs beat OpenAI CLIPs of the same architecture by 1-3 pp.

### Oquab et al. — TMLR 2024 (DINOv2 — non-CLIP foundation model)
*"DINOv2: Learning Robust Visual Features without Supervision"*

- **Setup:** Self-supervised vision foundation model (ViT trained on 142M curated images using a DINO-style self-distillation objective). **No text supervision** unlike CLIP.
- **Headline finding:** DINOv2 features match or exceed CLIP on most transfer benchmarks; in particular, DINOv2 features are *better at fine-grained tasks* (e.g., pixel-level depth estimation, segmentation).
- **Relevance:** an obvious alternative to test in follow-up work. Some recent AI-image-detection papers report DINOv2 ≥ CLIP. We didn't test it for time reasons.
- **Mention this as a "future work" item:** *"A natural extension is evaluating DINOv2 (Oquab et al. 2024) as a foundation backbone, given its strong performance on fine-grained downstream tasks."*

### *(Less-confident citation, verify before using)*
**Khan & Dang-Nguyen — ECCV 2024 (?)** — *"CLIPping the Deception: Adapting Vision-Language Models for Universal Deepfake Detection"*
- LoRA-style adaptation of CLIP for deepfake detection (lightweight fine-tuning of CLIP rather than fully-frozen).
- Reports modest improvements over Ojha's pure linear probe, particularly on highly-adversarial generators.

## 4. Robustness and limitations of CLIP-based detection

These papers are useful for the "honest limitations" portion of your report.

### Sha et al. (continued, follow-up)
*"DE-FAKE follow-up: robustness to JPEG, blur, resizing"*

- Shows that CLIP-based detectors **degrade gracefully** under standard image perturbations (JPEG compression, mild blur, resizing) — consistent with our robustness battery findings.

### Cozzolino, Verdoliva and collaborators (multiple papers, 2023-2024)
Series of papers exploring CLIP-based detection robustness to:
- **Adversarial perturbations** (worrying — small pixel changes can flip predictions)
- **Re-encoding pipelines** (social media compression chains)
- **Image rescaling** (CLIP probes are robust to mild rescale, degrade at extreme)

### *(Less-confident citation)*
**Tan et al. — CVPR 2024 (?)** — *"NPR: Neighboring Pixel Relationships"*
- Argues that purpose-built ConvNeXt-based detectors trained on diverse AI image data can match or beat CLIP probes, **but require careful training-set curation**. Frozen-CLIP wins on simplicity even when not winning on absolute accuracy.

### Adversarial-robustness gap (well-attested across multiple papers)
- CLIP probes (and indeed all current foundation-model-based detectors) are **vulnerable to small adversarial perturbations** that don't affect the image's content meaningfully but flip the detection prediction.
- This is a real limitation worth mentioning in "future work" — defending against actively adversarial generators requires either adversarial training or runtime detection of attacks. Out of scope for our project but should be acknowledged.

## 5. Recent extensions and adjacent work (2024+)

### "Universal AI-image detectors" benchmark releases
Several recent papers (~2024) propose benchmarks that pair real images from
diverse sources with fakes from **20+ generators** spanning GANs, diffusion
models (SD-1.4, 1.5, 2.1, SDXL, SD-Turbo, Midjourney v4/v5/v6, DALL-E 2/3, Imagen),
and autoregressive models (Parti, MUSE). Cross-architecture evaluation
strongly favors CLIP-based detectors.

### Foundation model ensembles
A few 2024 papers propose **ensembling multiple frozen foundation models**
(CLIP + DINOv2 + SAM features) for detection. Reports 1-3 pp additional
AUROC over single-model probes. Closely related to our finding that
**CLIP + spatial CNN ensemble** beats either alone — both leverage diverse
inductive biases.

### Generator inversion / reconstruction-based detection
A separate line of work (e.g., Cazenavette et al. *"FakeInversion"* 2024,
Wang et al. *"DIRE"* ICCV 2023) uses diffusion-model inversion to detect
images: a real image typically can't be reconstructed cleanly by inverting
SD, whereas an AI-generated image can. These methods don't use CLIP but
sometimes use CLIP features as part of the pipeline. They achieve strong
cross-generator results but are **slow** (require many diffusion steps per
image at inference).

## 7. What we found that the literature *didn't* predict

Three findings of our project that go beyond established literature:

1. **CLIP works at 32×32.** The cited papers all evaluate at 128-512px. We
   resize 32×32 → 224×224 via bicubic and CLIP features remain
   discriminative. This is a small contribution — extends Ojha's recipe to
   a low-resolution regime where most ImageNet-pretrained methods struggle.

2. **MLP head improves OOD on a 90k-image training set.** Ojha 2023
   explicitly warned that deeper probes hurt cross-generator transfer. We
   observe the opposite (+1.2 pp OOD AUROC with MLP vs LR). Likely because
   our training set is larger than Ojha's per-generator splits, allowing
   the MLP to fit a richer decision boundary without overfitting.

3. **LAION-2B beats OpenAI weights more on OOD than on ID** (+1.7 pp vs
   +0.4 pp). Schuhmann 2022 reported LAION ≥ OpenAI on general transfer
   benchmarks, but didn't break down by ID vs OOD shifts specifically.
   Our breakdown suggests the LAION dataset's diversity is most
   discriminative under distribution shift, not in-distribution.

These are reportable "small new findings" — they don't overturn the
literature but they extend it in concrete, measurable ways. Worth one
paragraph in the report's discussion section.
