## Cross-generator detection and the OOD baseline

### Wang, Wang, Owens, Efros — CVPR 2020
*"CNN-generated images are surprisingly easy to spot... for now"*

- A single ResNet-50 binary classifier trained on **ProGAN** images only,
  then evaluated on 10 unrelated generators (BigGAN, StyleGAN, CycleGAN,
  StarGAN, GauGAN, CRN, IMLE, SITD, SAN, deepfake portraits).
- With aggressive train-time augmentation (JPEG, blur, cropping), the
  ProGAN-trained detector generalizes to most other generators with >90% AP.
  Without augmentation, cross-generator AP drops to 50–60% on many generators.
- Introduces the **ForenSynths benchmark** that is still the standard
  cross-generator evaluation protocol.

### Frank et al. — ICML 2020
*"Leveraging Frequency Analysis for Deep Fake Image Recognition"*

- DCT-based features + simple classifier on GAN images.
- GAN upsampling produces grid-like artifacts visible in the DCT spectrum;
  a classifier on DCT coefficients separates real/fake with >99% accuracy
  in-distribution.
- These results are at 128×128+ resolution where the upsampling-grid peaks
  are well-resolved. At 32×32 (CIFAKE) the artifacts are aliased and the
  signal is much weaker. This is why our Model 4 trails the spatial models.

### Corvi, Cozzolino, Zingarini, Poggi, Nagano, Verdoliva — ICASSP 2023
*"On the detection of synthetic images generated by diffusion models"*

- Tests existing GAN-detection methods on diffusion-generated images (LDM,
  GLIDE, DALL-E 2).
- Diffusion models leave fingerprints similar to GANs but softer and more
  sensitive to post-processing (JPEG, blur). Existing GAN-detection methods
  transfer poorly without retraining.
- Matches what we see in the Model 4 robustness battery (blur and rescale
  collapse the spectral signal).

## CLIP for fake-image detection

### Ojha, Li, Lee — CVPR 2023
*"Towards Universal Fake Image Detectors that Generalize Across Generative Models"*

- Frozen CLIP ViT-L/14 image encoder + linear logistic regression head
  trained on fake images from ProGAN only. Evaluated on 19 generators
  (GANs + diffusion).
- Frozen CLIP + linear probe gets ~95% mean AP across the 19 generators;
  Wang et al. 2020 (ResNet-50 + heavy augmentation) gets ~78%.
- Three related findings: nearest-neighbour on CLIP features works almost
  as well as the linear probe; adding capacity to the probe (deeper MLPs)
  hurt their cross-generator transfer; ImageNet-pretrained backbones
  underperform CLIP when used the same way.
- All their experiments are at ≥256×256 and train on ProGAN only. We test
  at 32×32, train on SD-1.4, and use LAION-2B weights — this is the direct
  recipe Model 5 builds on.

### Cozzolino, Poggi, Corvi, Nießner, Verdoliva — CVPRW 2024
*"Raising the Bar of AI-generated Image Detection with CLIP"*

- Direct successor to Ojha 2023. Frozen CLIP + linear or shallow MLP probe,
  trained on a mix of GAN + diffusion images.
- Reports >95% mean accuracy across 19 generators including SD-XL and
  Midjourney v5. Notes that any amount of CLIP fine-tuning tends to hurt
  cross-generator transfer (a vote for the frozen-encoder choice).

### Sha, Li, Li, Zhang, Zhang — ACM CCS 2023
*"DE-FAKE: Detection and Attribution of Fake Images Generated by Text-to-Image Generation Models"*

- Uses CLIP image and text encoders to both detect AI images and attribute
  them to the specific generator (SD vs LDM vs GLIDE vs DALL-E).
- Achieves >90% detection AUROC and >80% attribution accuracy across
  4 text-to-image generators on the DE-FAKE dataset they release.

## Why CLIP works (and the obvious alternative)

### Radford et al. — ICML 2021
*"Learning Transferable Visual Models From Natural Language Supervision"*
The CLIP paper. 400M image-text pairs scraped from the web, contrastive
objective. Features transfer to 30+ downstream tasks without fine-tuning.

### Schuhmann et al. — NeurIPS 2022
*"LAION-5B: An open large-scale dataset for training next generation image-text models"*
Released LAION-5B, a 5B image-text dataset. CLIP models trained on its 2B
subset match or exceed OpenAI's original CLIP (which used 400M proprietary
pairs). We use LAION-2B weights for Model 5 — the +1.7 pp OOD lift over
the OpenAI baseline (see `04_clip_capacity_ladder.png`) is consistent
with their general transfer-benchmark numbers.

### Oquab et al. — TMLR 2024
*"DINOv2: Learning Robust Visual Features without Supervision"*
Self-supervised vision foundation model (no text supervision, unlike CLIP).
Matches or exceeds CLIP on most transfer benchmarks, particularly fine-grained
tasks. The natural alternative backbone to test next; we did not have time.

## Frequency / spectral detection (Model 4 motivation)

### Durall, Keuper, Keuper — CVPR 2020
*"Watching the Big Artifacts: Exposing DeepFake Videos by Detecting Face Warping Artifacts"*
(Note: their related 2020 paper *"Watch Your Up-Convolution"* is the more
cited one for radial-PSD analysis. Either is the standard citation for
"GANs fail to reproduce the radial power spectrum of natural images.")

### Dzanic, Shah, Witherden — NeurIPS 2020
*"Fourier Spectrum Discrepancies in Deep Network Generated Images"*
Quantifies that the high-frequency decay rate differs between real and
generated images, and that a few spectral statistics suffice to separate
them. Direct justification for the handcrafted Variant A feature set
(radial PSD + azimuthal sectors + scalar summaries).

## Robustness and limitations

### Cozzolino, Verdoliva and collaborators (multiple papers, 2023–2024)
Show that CLIP-based detectors degrade gracefully under standard image
perturbations (JPEG, mild blur, rescale) but remain vulnerable to small
adversarial perturbations. Consistent with our Model 5 robustness numbers
(strong on blur and JPEG, weaker on extreme noise).

### Adversarial-robustness gap (well-attested across multiple papers)
All current foundation-model-based detectors, including CLIP probes, are
vulnerable to small adversarial perturbations that flip the prediction
without meaningfully changing the image content. Defending against this
needs adversarial training or runtime detection of attacks — out of scope
here, listed in future work.

## Recent benchmarks and adjacent work

- **Universal AI-image detector benchmarks (2024+).** Several recent
  releases pair real images with fakes from 20+ generators spanning GANs,
  diffusion (SD-1.4 through SDXL, Midjourney v4–v6, DALL-E 2/3, Imagen),
  and autoregressive models (Parti, MUSE). Cross-architecture evaluation
  consistently favours CLIP-based detectors.
- **Foundation-model ensembles.** A few 2024 papers ensemble multiple
  frozen foundation models (CLIP + DINOv2 + SAM features) and report 1–3 pp
  additional AUROC over single-model probes. Related to our finding that
  ensembling CLIP with a spatial CNN beats either alone.
- **Generator-inversion methods.** Wang et al. *"DIRE"* ICCV 2023 and
  Cazenavette et al. *"FakeInversion"* 2024 use diffusion-model inversion
  to detect images (real images are harder to reconstruct cleanly via SD
  inversion than AI-generated ones). Strong cross-generator results but
  slow at inference (many diffusion steps per image).
